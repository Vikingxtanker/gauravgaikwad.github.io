About Pharmacy Practice Department

Department of Pharmacy Practice
The Department of Pharmacy Practice is an academic and clinical unit within pharmacy colleges dedicated to advancing patient-centered care through education, research, and service. Its primary focus is to train future pharmacists in clinical pharmacy—the application of pharmacotherapy knowledge directly at the patient’s bedside. Faculty in this department are licensed pharmacists with specialized training in clinical practice areas such as internal medicine, cardiology, critical care, pediatrics, oncology, and infectious diseases.

The department emphasizes evidence-based practice, ensuring students learn to optimize drug therapy, prevent medication errors, and improve health outcomes. Students gain exposure to hospital and community pharmacy practice, clinical ward rounds, drug information services, and pharmacovigilance. Interprofessional collaboration is central, with pharmacy faculty working alongside physicians, nurses, and other health professionals.

Research within pharmacy practice focuses on clinical outcomes, rational drug use, antimicrobial stewardship, and pharmacoeconomics. In addition, the department contributes to continuing education for practicing pharmacists and supports health systems in medication policy and patient safety.
